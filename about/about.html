our activities

apprenticeship program

The Apprenticeship Program is for students who opted to learn a trade rather than a college degree. The student works with a master for several years until they are deemed qualified and skilled enough to practice their trade professionally. The required procedure is to pay the master or madam a set fee at the beginning of the training, and again for graduation. For example, when Sensible Seth began the auto mechanic apprenticeship program, his master requested 70 Ghana cedis, two bottles of schnapps, two bottles of minerals, six packets of matches, two bottles of beer, six packets of cigarettes, two bottles of gin, and six bottles of malt. In order for Seth to officially complete the program and receive his certificate, and as is customary in Ghana, Sethâ€™s master requested four bottles of gin, one crate of minerals, two boxes of matches, one sheep, and two fowls with cooking utensils. mobility program

The mobility program began long ago when I first saw Nukuku crawling on the ground in Dalive-Torzikpota Village. Since then we have provided wheelchairs and walkers to people with disabilities (a 4 yr old boy with cerebral palsy, a young man who was trapped when a building collapsed on him, and many more.) Because of the rough terrain, wheelchairs need to be replaced often. Over the years, we have replaced Nukuku;s chair several times. This is the most recent one which was custom fit for him with support for his legs, and a reading/eating table.
